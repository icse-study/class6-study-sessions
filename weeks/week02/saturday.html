⚡ Core Definitions

Matter · Molecule · Intermolecular

Matter — has mass + occupies space (NOT: light, sound, heat, shadow).
Molecule — smallest independent particle retaining all properties.
Intermolecular Space: Solid < Liquid < Gas.
Intermolecular Force: Solid > Liquid > Gas.

Cohesion vs Adhesion

Cohesion — same substance (water–water, iron–iron).
Adhesion — different substances (water–glass, paint–wall).

🌬️ Diffusion · 🔥 Heat · 💨 Air

Key Rules to Remember

Diffusion — spontaneous mixing; fastest in gases (large spaces + fast motion).
Heating → molecules speed up → substance expands.
Cooling → molecules slow down → substance contracts.
Air is matter — has mass (balloon gets heavier) and occupies space (dry tissue experiment).

💡 Hard Question Hint
Sponge compressibility: the air in pores compresses, not the solid.
Water with iron's force: can't flow → behaves like a solid.
No intermolecular space: no movement possible → everything becomes rigid.
