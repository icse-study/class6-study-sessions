🏛️ The Indus Valley Civilisation

One of the world's oldest civilisations (3300–1300 BCE), found along the Indus River.

  • Major cities: Mohenjo-daro and Harappa
  • Known for advanced town planning, drainage systems, and trade
  • Declined around 1900 BCE (reasons debated — floods, drought, migration)
3300 BCE → 2600 BCE (peak) → 1900 BCE (decline)

⚡ Motion — Speed, Distance & Time

When something moves, it covers a distance in a time. How fast = speed.

Speed = Distance ÷ Time

Example: Car travels 120 km in 2 hours → Speed = 60 km/h

Rearranged:

D = S × T  |  T = D ÷ S

⚗️ States of Matter

All matter exists in three states:

  • Solid — fixed shape and volume (e.g. ice)
  • Liquid — fixed volume, no fixed shape (e.g. water)
  • Gas — no fixed shape or volume (e.g. steam)
Solid ⟶ Liquid (Melting) ⟶ Gas (Evaporation)

🧬 The Cell — Basic Unit of Life

Every living thing is made of cells. Key parts:

  • Nucleus — control centre, contains DNA
  • Cell membrane — controls what enters/exits
  • Mitochondria — powerhouse, makes energy
  • Chloroplast — only in plant cells, for photosynthesis
6CO₂ + 6H₂O + light → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂
