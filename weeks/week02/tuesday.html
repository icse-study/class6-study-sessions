⚡ Physics · Chapter 1 · Matter
Everything around you is matter!
Matter is anything that has mass and occupies space. Books, water, air, stone — all matter. Light, sound, heat, shadow and thoughts are not matter. Study these cards then take the quiz!
🧱 What is Matter?

Matter

Anything that has mass and occupies space.

Is matter: books, water, air, stone, iron
NOT matter: light, sound, heat, shadow, thoughts
💡 If you can weigh it and it takes up room → it's matter

Molecule

The smallest independent particle of a substance that retains all the properties of that substance. Cannot be seen with the naked eye. Always in motion.

💡 Tiny building block that still 'knows' what it is
🔗 Intermolecular Properties

Intermolecular Space

The gap between molecules in a substance. Determines how easily a substance can be compressed or flow.

Solid: very small  ·  Liquid: moderate  ·  Gas: very large
💡 Solid < Liquid < Gas (space increases)

Intermolecular Force (Attraction)

The attractive force between molecules. Stronger forces hold molecules closer together.

Solid: strong  ·  Liquid: moderate  ·  Gas: very weak

Cohesion vs Adhesion

Cohesion — attraction between molecules of the same substance.
Adhesion — attraction between molecules of different substances.

Cohesion: water–water, iron–iron  ·  Adhesion: water–glass, paint–wall
💡 CO-hesion = same COmpany  ·  AD-hesion = ADding different things
🧊💧💨 States of Matter

Solid

Fixed shape and fixed volume. Molecules tightly packed with strong forces. Very hard to compress.

Examples: wood, stone, ice, metal

Liquid

Fixed volume, no fixed shape — takes the shape of the container. Molecules less packed, can slide past each other. Can flow.

Examples: water, milk, oil

Gas

No fixed shape, no fixed volume — fills the entire container. Molecules far apart, very weak forces. Highly compressible.

Examples: air, oxygen, CO₂

🌬️ Diffusion · 🔥 Heat Effects · 💨 Air is Matter

Diffusion

The spontaneous mixing of particles due to random molecular motion — no fan or stirring needed.

Speed: gases (fastest) → liquids → solids (slowest)
Example: perfume spreading across a room
💡 Perfume spreading → no fan needed!

Effect of Heat & Cooling

Heating → molecules move faster → intermolecular spaces increase → substance expands.
Cooling → molecules slow down → spaces decrease → substance contracts.

💡 Hot = fast + far apart  ·  Cold = slow + close together

Air is Matter

Air has mass and occupies space, so it is matter.

Experiment: Push an inverted glass straight down into water — tissue inside stays dry because air occupies the space.
Also: A balloon gets heavier when blown up (air has mass).
💡 Exam Tip — Remember: Solid → Liquid → Gas
Intermolecular Space: Low → Med → High
Force of Attraction: High → Med → Low
Compressibility: Low → Med → High
Diffusion Rate: Low → Med → High
