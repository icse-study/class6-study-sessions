Common -ER verbs

parler (speak) · aimer (like) · regarder (watch) · écouter (listen) · habiter (live) · étudier (study) · visiter (visit) · jouer (play) · dessiner (draw) · chanter (sing) · préférer (prefer) · préparer (prepare) · marcher (walk) · déjeuner (have lunch).

💡 For -ger verbs (manger, voyager) keep the 'e' before -ons: nous mangeons (soft 'g').

📦 Articles & Negation

Definite articles (the)

le — masculine singular (le livre) · la — feminine singular (la table) · l' — before a vowel or silent h (l'école, l'homme) · les — plural (les livres).

Making it negative: ne … pas

Put ne before the verb and pas after it. Before a vowel, ne → n'.

Je ne parle pas français. · Nous n'aimons pas la grammaire.

18. What is the difference between 'tu' and 'vous'? When do we use each?
▸ Show model answer
'tu' is informal singular — for friends and family. 'vous' is formal singular OR plural — for strangers, teachers, or several people.
19. Give the French words for 5 body parts that come in pairs (two of each).
▸ Show model answer
les yeux (eyes), les oreilles (ears), les bras (arms), les jambes (legs), les mains (hands).
