Liquids, Gases & Changes of State
How liquids and gases behave, and how matter changes state when heated. Read the cards, then take the quiz.
💧 Liquids

Properties of liquids

Weak intermolecular force, more space than solids, molecules free to move within a boundary → no definite shape (takes the container's), definite volume, almost incompressible, can flow.

Viscosity: a liquid's opposition to flow (honey > water). Surface tension: pulls molecules inward → liquids form spherical drops. Liquids have one free surface.
💨 Gases

Properties of gases

Negligible intermolecular force, very large space, molecules move freely everywhere → no definite shape or volume, highly compressible, very fast diffusion, no free surfaces.

💡 Gas pressure = molecules constantly colliding with the container walls.
🔥 Changes of State

Melting & Boiling

Solid → Liquid (melting/fusion): heat lets molecules overcome forces and move freely. Melting point of ice = 0°C.
Liquid → Gas (boiling/vaporisation): heat makes molecules move fast and escape. Boiling point of water = 100°C.
