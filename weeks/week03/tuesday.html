Matter, its particles & solids
Matter is anything that occupies space and has mass. Learn what it is made of, how its particles behave, and the properties of solids. Read the cards, then take the quiz.
🧱 What is Matter?

Matter

Anything that occupies space and has mass. It can be sensed (smell, touch, sight…). Everything living and non-living is made of matter.

Examples: air, water, sugar, sand, iron, wood, ice, milk, oil, food, plants, animals.

Atom vs Molecule

Atom — the smallest particle of matter. Molecule — the smallest particle of a substance that can exist freely in nature (made of one or more atoms). Molecules are extremely small: about 10⁻⁹ m to 10⁻¹⁰ m.

💡 A single drop of water holds hundreds of millions of atoms!
🔎 Properties of Matter Particles

The four key properties

1. Very small: a few KMnO₄ crystals colour a huge volume of water on repeated dilution.
2. Spaces between them: 20 g salt dissolves in 100 ml water without raising the level — salt fills the inter-molecular space.
3. Constant random motion: dust dances in sunlight; perfume spreads across a room (zig-zag motion).
4. Attract each other: the intermolecular force is strongest in solids, less in liquids, least in gases.

Cohesive vs Adhesive force

Cohesive — attraction between particles of the same substance. Adhesive — attraction between different substances.

Water sticks to glass (adhesive > cohesive). Mercury does NOT stick to glass (adhesive < cohesive).
🧊 States of Matter & Solids

Three states (+ plasma)

Matter exists as solid, liquid and gas (water can be all three: ice, water, steam). Plasma is the fourth state, found at very high temperature. The state depends on inter-molecular space, force of attraction and molecular motion.

Solids

Strong intermolecular force, negligible space, molecules only vibrate about fixed positions → definite shape and volume, cannot be compressed, cannot flow, highly rigid and dense.

💡 A rubber band changes shape but springs back — it is elastic, yet still a solid.
