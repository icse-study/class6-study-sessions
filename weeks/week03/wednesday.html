Continuous, Future, Modals & Imperatives
Harder passive structures: continuous tenses ("being"), future & modal verbs ("be"), and command/request sentences. Read the cards, then take the quiz.
⏳ Continuous Tenses — add "being"

The "being" rule

For continuous tenses (verb + -ing), keep the continuous feel by adding being before V₃.

Present continuous: is/am/are + being + V₃ — The artist is drawing a sketch → A sketch is being drawn by the artist.
Past continuous: was/were + being + V₃ — They were cleaning the rooms → The rooms were being cleaned by them.

🔮 Future & Modals — add "be"

The "be" rule

For future (will/shall) or modals (can, must, should, may): will / modal + be + V₃.

The principal will announce the winners → The winners will be announced by the principal.
You must submit the assignment → The assignment must be submitted by you.

💡 Modals always keep a "be" in the passive.
❗ Imperative Sentences
TypePassive formulaExample
CommandLet + object + be + V₃Shut the windows → Let the windows be shut.
RequestYou are requested to …Please stand in a line → You are requested to stand in a line.
AdviceYou are advised to …Respect your elders → Let your elders be respected.
