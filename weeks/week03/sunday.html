⛰️ Mountains — 3 Types

Fold Mountains

Tectonic plates collide → crust crumples into folds. Most common type.

Examples: Himalayas · Alps · Andes

Block Mountains

Crust cracks along faults → block is uplifted or surrounding land sinks.

Examples: Vindhyas (India) · Black Forest (Germany) · Vosges (France)

Volcanic Mountains

Built from lava, ash and rocks ejected during volcanic eruptions.

Examples: Mt. Kilimanjaro (Africa) · Mt. Fujiyama (Japan)
🏔️ Valley · 🏜️ Plateau · 🌾 Plains

Rift Valley

Central block slips down between two parallel faults → long, low-lying depression.

Examples: Rhine River Valley · Narmada Valley (India)

Plateau / Tableland

Elevated, broad, flat area rising sharply above surroundings. Rich in minerals.

Examples: Deccan Plateau (India) · Tibet Plateau · East African Plateau

Plains

Vast flat lowland formed by river sediment deposition. Very fertile — high population density.

Examples: Gangetic Plains (India) · Great Plains (North America)
⚡ Forces · 👨‍👩‍👧 People

Endogenic vs Exogenic

Endogenic (internal) — BUILD landforms: plate collision, faulting.
Exogenic (external) — SHAPE landforms: rivers, wind, erosion.

Life in Plains vs Mountains

Plains: High population · easy transport · fertile farming land.
Mountains: Low population · difficult farming · important for tourism & rivers.

Talking about aches — j'ai mal à / aux …

J'ai mal à la tête (I have a headache) · J'ai mal aux genoux (my knees hurt) · J'ai mal aux mains (my hands hurt) · J'ai mal au dos (my back hurts).

💡 à + le → au · à + les → aux · à + la stays à la.

➕ Plurals & Accents

Making sentences plural

Add -s to nouns, change articles (le/la → les), add -s to adjectives, and use the plural verb form.

Singular: Nicolas habite à Lyon. → Plural: Nicolas et Pierre habitent à Lyon.

16. Put this dialogue in the correct order: "Merci beaucoup" / "Non, je suis au sixième." / "Salut ! Je suis Paul. Et toi ?" / "Je m'appelle Éric." / "Tu as un beau visage."
▸ Show model answer
1) Salut ! Je suis Paul. Et toi ? 2) Je m'appelle Éric. 3) Tu as un beau visage. 4) Merci beaucoup. 5) Non, je suis au sixième.
17. Describe Anne's friend Claire using at least 4 characteristics from the text.
▸ Show model answer
Claire is Anne's friend. She is French. She has long black hair and blue eyes. She is beautiful and very intelligent.
18. Write a short paragraph (4–5 sentences) describing a family member.
▸ Show model answer
Sample: Ma mère s'appelle Sophie. Elle a les cheveux longs et bruns. Elle a les yeux verts. Elle est mince et grande. Elle a un joli sourire.
19. What is the rule for making a sentence negative in French? Give an example.
▸ Show model answer
Put ne before the verb and pas after it (ne → n' before a vowel). Example: "Je parle français" → "Je ne parle pas français."
20. Translate to French: (a) I have a headache. (b) They are Italian. (c) We have a nice garden.
▸ Show model answer
(a) J'ai mal à la tête. (b) Ils sont italiens. (or Elles sont italiennes for all girls) (c) Nous avons un beau jardin.
